**Full voiceover:**
"A tumor is made of living cells. Living cells need oxygen and nutrients. Past a tiny size, diffusion is not enough. Tumors release signals, especially VEGF, that recruit new blood vessels. That process is angiogenesis: the tumor building supply lines. Anti-angiogenic therapy tries to interrupt that system. But tumors adapt, vessels normalize or reroute, and simple starvation stories get complicated. Angiogenesis is a perfect cancer medicine lesson: the target is real, but biology routes around simple stories."
